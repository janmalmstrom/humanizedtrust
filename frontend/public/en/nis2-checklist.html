Is your organisation NIS2 ready?

10 questions. 4 areas. Immediate result. Identify your biggest gaps before the NCSC inspection.

Governance & Accountability
1
Has the board formally approved a cybersecurity policy? NIS2 requires the management body to formally approve and oversee cybersecurity measures. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Microsoft Secure Score gives the board a single number (0–100) that proves compliance status. Ask your IT manager.
2
Is there a designated NIS2 responsible person with the mandate to act? One person (CISO, IT manager or equivalent) who owns the issue. 🔧 Microsoft tool: The NIS2 responsible can monitor status via Microsoft Defender portal — no technical background required.
Risk Management
3
Do you have an updated IT risk register (max 12 months old)? Identified threats, probabilities and consequences documented. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Microsoft Purview Compliance Manager gives you a ready-made NIS2 risk register — connected to your actual M365 configuration.
4
Have you implemented MFA for all users? Email, business systems, cloud services — MFA is a minimum requirement. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Enable Security Defaults in Entra ID — free, takes 10 minutes, and prevents 99.9% of account attacks.
5
Do you have systematic patch management for critical vulnerabilities? Known vulnerabilities must be addressed systematically. A documented schedule is required. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Microsoft Intune automates patching for all Windows devices — critical patches within 48h without manual work.
Incident Handling
6
Do you have a documented incident response plan? Step by step: who does what, who communicates, how do we log it. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Microsoft Sentinel (SIEM) automates incident detection and provides ready templates for incident documentation.
7
Can you report a security incident to NCSC within 24 hours? Early warning must be reported within 24 hours — do you know to whom? 🔧 Microsoft tool: Defender XDR gives you a complete incident timeline in minutes — exactly what NCSC requires in the initial report.
8
Do you have continuous logging of your critical systems? Logs must be kept long enough to investigate an incident retrospectively. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Unified Audit Log in Microsoft 365 — enable now for free. Logs all activity for 90–365 days.
Technical Measures
9
Is sensitive data encrypted both at rest and in transit? Encryption is the last line of defence and an explicit NIS2 requirement. 🔧 Microsoft tool: BitLocker (laptops) + SharePoint encryption + TLS for email — all included in M365 Business Premium.
10
Do you have tested backups with verified recovery procedures? A backup you haven't tested is no backup — document the most recent test. 🔧 Microsoft tool: Azure Backup automates backup and lets you test recovery without affecting production — documented proof for NCSC.
