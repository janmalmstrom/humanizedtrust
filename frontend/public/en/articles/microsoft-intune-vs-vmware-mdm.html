Device Control & MDM

Microsoft Intune vs VMware Workspace ONE: MDM for NIS2 Compliance

Published 19 April 2026 · Reading time: 8 minutes · For: CEOs and Board Members
Summary
  • NIS2 Article 21 requires documented device control — who can access what, from where, and with what level of authentication.
  • Mobile Device Management (MDM) is not optional for essential and important entities. It is a compliance requirement.
  • Two leading solutions compete in the Irish market: Microsoft Intune (integrated with Microsoft 365) and VMware Workspace ONE (independent deployment).
  • For a 50–500 person Irish company, the choice depends on your existing infrastructure, audit reporting needs, and board risk tolerance.
  • Your board is personally liable under NIS2 Regulation 32. You need to document which solution you chose and why.
Bottom Line

The right MDM tool is not the one with the most features. It is the one your board can defend in a regulatory audit, with complete audit logs and conditional access policies documented and approved.

Why MDM Matters for NIS2 — Article 21 Device Control

Article 21 of the NIS2 Directive lists ten essential security measures. The second on that list is access control and device management. It is not a suggestion. It is a legal requirement for any essential or important entity in Ireland.

The requirement states that you must be able to:

  • Identify and authenticate all devices accessing your systems
  • Enforce multi-factor authentication (MFA) before access is granted
  • Restrict device features based on security policy (disable USB ports, require disk encryption, enforce app whitelisting)
  • Block access from non-compliant or compromised devices in real time
  • Audit all device access attempts — successful and failed — for 90 days minimum
  • Revoke access instantly if a device is lost, stolen, or compromised

An MDM solution is the technical foundation that enables this. Without one, you cannot prove to the NCSC Ireland (or your sector regulator) that you have implemented Article 21 controls.

Your board needs to know: If NCSC Ireland conducts a compliance audit and finds that devices can access corporate data without MDM enrollment, that is a direct violation of Article 21. Penalties are up to €10 million or 2% of global annual turnover for essential entities — whichever is higher. Board members can be held personally liable under Regulation 32.

Intune: The Integrated Path

Microsoft Intune is a cloud-native MDM service built into the Microsoft 365 ecosystem. If your organisation is already using Microsoft 365 for email, Teams, and Office apps, Intune is the path of least resistance.

Why Intune Works Well for NIS2

  • Conditional Access Integration: Intune connects directly to Azure AD (now Entra ID). When a user tries to log in to Teams, Outlook, or SharePoint, Entra ID checks: "Is this device enrolled in Intune? Is it compliant with our policies?" If not, access is denied. No separate integration effort.
  • Automatic Logging: Every device enrollment, every policy push, every compliance check, and every access denial is logged to Azure Audit Log automatically. The NCSC Ireland auditor can request this log directly without you needing to export data manually.
  • BYOD Enforcement: Employees can enroll personal devices (phones, tablets, laptops). Intune creates a management container that isolates corporate data from personal apps. An employee can use the same phone for work and personal life; Intune only controls the work partition. This is a critical Article 21 control because personal devices are often the weakest link.
  • Real-Time Threat Response: If Intune detects that a device is compromised (e.g., jailbroken iOS, rooted Android, or missing security updates), it can instantly revoke access to corporate email and cloud services without requiring any manual intervention.

Intune Trade-Offs

  • Less control on-premises: Intune is cloud-native. If you have legacy on-premises applications (Windows servers, file shares, databases), Intune alone cannot enforce policies on those. You need additional integration with on-premises identity systems.
  • Vendor lock-in: Committing to Intune means accepting Microsoft as your MDM provider. Switching later is costly. If your board wants flexibility or multi-vendor risk distribution, this is a consideration.
  • Dependent on Azure infrastructure: If Azure has an outage (rare, but it happens), your MDM is affected. Enterprise Service Level Agreements mitigate this risk, but it is not zero.

Board Talking Point — Intune

"Intune is the integrated choice. It requires minimal additional tooling, reduces operational complexity, and provides automatic audit trails that satisfy NCSC requirements. We avoid the complexity of integrating a separate MDM with our Microsoft stack, which reduces implementation risk and lowers TCO."

VMware Workspace ONE: The Independent Path

VMware Workspace ONE is a comprehensive Enterprise Mobility Management (EMM) platform. It is deployed independently of any specific cloud provider and can manage devices across Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, and even hybrid VDI environments.

Why Workspace ONE Works Well for NIS2

  • Vendor Independence: Workspace ONE does not lock you into Microsoft infrastructure. If you use a mix of Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Salesforce, or custom applications, Workspace ONE can enforce policy across all of them equally.
  • Advanced Conditional Access: Workspace ONE offers granular conditional policies based on device location, network type, time of day, and threat score. You can write rules like: "Only allow access to financial systems from enrolled devices on corporate network between 7 AM and 7 PM."
  • Identity Bridge: Workspace ONE integrates with your identity provider (Active Directory, Okta, Azure AD, etc.). It acts as a neutral arbiter between devices and your corporate apps, regardless of where those apps live.
  • Hybrid Flexibility: You can deploy Workspace ONE in your own data centre, on Azure, on AWS, or as SaaS. This gives you control over data residency — important for regulated Irish organisations that must keep data on Irish or EU servers.

Workspace ONE Trade-Offs

  • Higher complexity: Workspace ONE is more powerful but requires deeper configuration. You need a dedicated architect or integrator to set it up correctly. Implementation timelines are typically 12–16 weeks for a 200-person organisation.
  • Higher cost: Per-device licensing plus infrastructure costs. For a 200-person organisation with personal devices, annual cost is typically €8,000–12,000, compared to €4,000–6,000 for Intune.
  • Conditional Access requires manual wiring: Unlike Intune, which integrates natively with Entra ID, Workspace ONE requires you to configure conditional access rules separately in your identity platform. This creates more potential for misconfiguration.
  • Integration with Microsoft 365 is indirect: Workspace ONE can enforce that a device is compliant before granting access to Microsoft 365 apps, but it does so via API integration, not native integration. This adds latency and requires more monitoring.

Board Talking Point — Workspace ONE

"Workspace ONE is the vendor-neutral choice. It provides strategic independence from Microsoft and works with any identity provider or application platform. We gain advanced conditional access control and data residency flexibility. The trade-off is complexity and cost, which we accept because our security posture requires this level of control."

BYOD Handling — A Critical NIS2 Requirement

Article 21 emphasises access control. Many breaches originate not from corporate-owned devices, but from employee personal phones, tablets, and home laptops. Both Intune and Workspace ONE address this, but differently.

Intune's Approach to BYOD

Intune uses the concept of a managed app container. When an employee enrolls a personal iOS device, Intune does not control the entire phone. Instead, it installs the Intune app and one or more corporate apps (Outlook, Teams, etc.). Intune monitors only those apps.

The benefit: Employee privacy is respected. The downside: Intune cannot see the device's overall health (jailbreak status, OS updates, etc.) unless the entire device is enrolled in a stricter "full management" mode — which most employees resist.

Workspace ONE's Approach to BYOD

Workspace ONE offers two modes: app-based (similar to Intune) and full device enrollment. For compliance, Workspace ONE recommends full device enrollment, which gives full visibility into device health, threat status, and policy compliance.

The benefit: Your board can document that you have complete visibility into every device accessing corporate data. The downside: Employees may resist full device enrollment on personal phones, which creates adoption friction.

NIS2 Audit Reality: An NCSC auditor will ask: "Can you show me the policy for employee personal devices? Can you prove devices are enrolled in MDM before accessing corporate data?" Both Intune and Workspace ONE can answer yes. The question is whether your BYOD enrollment rate is 60% or 95%. The higher rate is easier to defend.

Audit Reporting — The Board Must See This

NIS2 Article 21 requires that you maintain audit logs of device access for 90 days minimum. Regulation 32 holds board members personally liable if these logs cannot be produced during an audit.

What Does an Audit Log Look Like?

A compliant audit log shows:

  • Device name, operating system, and unique identifier (IMEI, serial number)
  • User identity
  • Timestamp of access attempt
  • Resource accessed (email, SharePoint, database)
  • Access decision (granted or denied)
  • Reason for denial (if denied) — e.g., "Device non-compliant: encryption disabled"

Intune Audit Reporting

Intune logs are stored in Azure Audit Log (part of Azure Monitor). You can query them via the Azure Portal or export them programmatically. Microsoft provides pre-built reports for common NIS2 questions:

  • "Which devices accessed our email system in the past 30 days?"
  • "Which devices are non-compliant with our encryption policy?"
  • "Show me all failed access attempts to Teams in the past 7 days."

The advantage: Minimal additional tooling. The logs are always there, always compliant, and always auditable.

Workspace ONE Audit Reporting

Workspace ONE has built-in reporting dashboards and can export logs to your SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) tool or cloud storage. VMware provides NIS2-specific report templates.

The advantage: You control where logs are stored and for how long. The disadvantage: You must set up log retention policies manually; if misconfigured, you may not have 90 days of logs when audited.

Real Scenario

Company: Mid-size Irish financial services firm, 150 employees.

They chose Intune because they were already a Microsoft 365 shop. In March 2026, a ransomware campaign targets their sector. NCSC Ireland reaches out to all companies in the sector asking for device access logs during the attack window (March 15–17).

The IT manager pulls Azure Audit Logs covering those three days. In under 10 minutes, they can show: 143 devices enrolled, 2 blocked because encryption was disabled, 1 device quarantined because it was detected communicating with a known C&C server. NCSC is satisfied. No further investigation.

Outcome: The company satisfied the Article 21 audit requirement in one IT hour. The board had documented evidence that controls were in place and working. No exposure. No fines.

If they had not had MDM in place, they would have had to say: "We don't know which devices accessed our systems. We can't prove device authentication." That is a direct Article 21 violation.
